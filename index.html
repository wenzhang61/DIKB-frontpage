ABOUT

The Drug Interaction Knowledge Base (DIKB) is an evidence-focused knowledge base designed to support pharmacoepidemiology and clinical decision support. The DIKB contains quantitative and qualitative assertions about drug mechanisms and pharmacokinetic drug-drug interactions for over 60 drugs; primarily psychotropics and HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors (statins).
This project is supported by a grant from the National Library of Medicine: "Addressing gaps in clinically useful evidence on drug-drug interactions"

   YOU MIGHT ALSO BE INTERESTED IN...
 

  • The University of Pittsburgh Linked Structured Product Label repository. This resource provides a Linked Open Data representation of Structured Product Labels (SPLs) for over 35,000 prescription and over-the-counter drug products. Each SPL is represented as an RDF graph that contains the raw text for each product label section, the NDF-RT drug class of each active ingredient in a drug product, and links to RxNorm, www.drugbank.ca/, and ChEBI.
